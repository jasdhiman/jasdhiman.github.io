Overview

Mentoring across research, design, and applied environmental systems

17 students

I have mentored, or assisted my research supervisors in mentoring, a total of 17 undergraduate and graduate students. Some of these students were mentored independently, while others were supported through collaborative supervision.

Research and design projects

These projects have spanned experimental research, field and watershed modelling, environmental analytics, engineering design, water quality, soil systems, climate impacts, and applied data science.

Student development

My goal is to help students move from task completion toward independent thinking, careful interpretation, strong communication, and professional confidence.

Student project areas

Types of projects mentored

Water quality and remediation

Projects have included water treatment, contaminant removal, biochar-amended filtration, wastewater reuse, and strategies for safer use of wastewater in agricultural systems.

Soil, nutrients, and crop systems

Mentored work has included nutrient leaching, soil amendments, soil fertility, greenhouse gas emissions, crop growth and yield, and phosphorus dynamics in agricultural soils.

Hydrology, climate, and geospatial analysis

Student projects have involved winter hydrology, phosphorus-loss modelling, surface hydrodynamic modelling, climate impacts, satellite-based watershed analysis, and GIS-supported environmental assessment.

Data science and environmental analytics

Projects have used data-driven approaches to analyze biochar sorption, agricultural practices, soil health, hydrological processes, and climate-resilient agricultural decision-making.

Community and partner-informed design

Some projects have involved applied design and environmental monitoring questions connected to community, watershed, and partner needs.

Professional skills

Mentorship also includes helping students develop literature review skills, project planning, data analysis, interpretation, presentation, writing, and confidence in communicating technical work.

Mentorship approach

How I mentor students

“I view mentoring as more than technical supervision. It includes helping students learn how to formulate questions, work through ambiguity, present their findings, and build confidence as emerging scholars and professionals.”
