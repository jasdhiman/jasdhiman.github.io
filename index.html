About

Environmental engineering, data-driven modelling, and student-centred teaching.

I am a Faculty Lecturer in the Department of Bioresource Engineering at McGill University. My academic work bridges environmental sustainability, bioresource engineering, agricultural water management, hydrological and climate modelling, and applied data analytics.

My research has focused on safe wastewater reuse in agriculture, biochar and hydrogel-based soil amendments, contaminant transport, water remediation, winter hydrology, climate impacts on agricultural systems, geospatial analysis, and machine learning applications for environmental prediction.

As an educator, I teach across computing, statistics, simulation and modelling, GIS, data analytics, physics, engineering science, and scientific communication. My teaching emphasizes clarity, applied examples, hands-on practice, and a supportive learning environment for students working with challenging quantitative and computational material.

Research

Research themes

My work connects experimental research, environmental modelling, geospatial tools, and applied data science to support more sustainable and resilient agricultural and environmental systems.

Wastewater reuse, soil amendments, and remediation

Evaluating biochar, hydrogels, and other soil amendments to reduce contaminant mobility, support crop production, and promote safer use of wastewater in agriculture.

Hydrological and climate modelling

Improving simulation of field- and watershed-scale processes, including winter hydrology, phosphorus loss, frost-free days, climate-sensitive agricultural processes, and Great Lakes agricultural watersheds.

Geospatial analysis and remote sensing

Using satellite imagery, GIS, and spatial modelling to characterize hydrological variability, soil erosion factors, land management, and environmental risk across agricultural landscapes.

Machine learning for environmental prediction

Applying statistical learning, predictive modelling, and data-driven methods to environmental systems, including rainfall characteristics, soil health, climate impacts, and model improvement.

Future research direction

My long-term goal is to build an applied, collaborative research program that integrates sustainable water management, climate-resilient agricultural systems, geospatial intelligence, and accessible computational tools for environmental decision-making.

Teaching

Teaching that makes quantitative and computational material approachable.

My teaching is guided by clarity, structure, applied learning, kindness, and high expectations. I aim to help students build confidence while developing transferable analytical and engineering skills.
