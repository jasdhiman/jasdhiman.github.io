Overview

Service philosophy

My service contributions reflect a commitment to supporting students, strengthening academic communities, and contributing to the broader profession. I have served in mentoring roles, participated in academic and professional committees, reviewed manuscripts for peer-reviewed journals, and contributed to interdisciplinary academic initiatives in Canada and internationally. These experiences have allowed me to support students and early-career researchers, engage with broader scholarly networks, and help create collaborative environments that extend beyond the classroom and laboratory.

I value service that is both constructive and outward-facing: mentoring emerging scholars, contributing to professional dialogue, supporting academic events and speaker series, and helping build partnerships across institutions and sectors. My service experiences have also reinforced the importance of leadership, collegiality, and community engagement in academic life. As I continue to develop as a faculty member, I see service not as a separate obligation, but as an integral part of sustaining strong programs, inclusive learning environments, and collaborative scholarly communities.

Workshop organization

Organizing international workshops

I conducted an international workshop, titled “Innovations in Sustainable Water Resource Management (ISWRM)” on 2nd July 2018 in Ludhiana, India (http://www.hqp-icimpacts.com/iswrm-2018.html). This workshop was led and organized by me, and the aim of the workshop was to disseminate information regarding innovative, low-tech and low-cost techniques to tackle the problem of declining water quality and water conservation. The workshop was attended by some of the identified stakeholders; experts from industry, government officials, farmers, students as well as professors and researchers from various universities in USA, Canada and India. Findings of the research along with other innovative technologies were also presented to the Chief Agriculture Officer (CAO) - Department of Agriculture, Government of Punjab, India, with an aim that the local government would consider the shared information while drafting policies concerning agricultural water use and quality. Assurance was provided by the government official, that the presented techniques and information would be utilized in developing government programs and policies in the future. The initiative of conducting the workshop on a very relevant and important topic was also lauded, and the event was covered by electronic and print media.

I served as “logistics manager” in the organizing committee for a one-day workshop on Sustainable Solid Waste Management (SUSWAM-2017), sponsored by IC-IMPACTS, held at McGill University, Canada on 19th June 2017. The purpose of this workshop was to gather people from the academics, communities and industries, and motivate them on entrepreneurial skills that will enable them to start their own business. My role was to ensure smooth and timely procurement of materials required for the workshop, as well as arranging for accommodation for participating speakers. The workshop was a success and participants benefitted from the presented information about resources helpful for commercialization of products and technologies related to solid waste management.
